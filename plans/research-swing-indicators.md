# Research on Technical Indicators for Stock Swing Trading\n\nRecent academic papers (2025-2026) from arXiv emphasize empirical backtests of technical indicators like RSI, MACD, Bollinger Bands for short-term (days to weeks) trading, aligning with swing strategies. Focus on nonlinear ML/DL models outperforming baselines. Below: summaries of 5 top papers with backtests.\n\n## 1. Deep Learning for Short-Term Equity Trend Forecasting: A Behavior-Driven Multi-Factor Approach\n**Authors:** Yuqi Luan et al.\n**Year:** 2025 (arXiv:2508.14656)\n**Key Findings:** Dual-task MLP on 40 behavioral alpha factors (incl. MACD, RSI, Bollinger Bands from price/volume) predicts 5-day returns/direction, capturing momentum herding, reversals, divergences. Outperforms CNN/SVM in IC, ICIR, Sharpe via long-short portfolios.\n**Indicators/Patterns:** RSI rebounds, MACD crosses, BB deviations × volume.\n**Performance:** Superior IR; top-K backtests show stable alpha.\n**Impl Notes:** Input z-scored factors → MLP (64-32 ReLU neurons, dropout 0.1) → dual heads (MSE + 0.5 BCE loss, Adam lr=5e-4). Formulas: Standard MLP forward pass, losses as eqs (4-5).\n**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.14656\n\n## 2. Deep Reinforcement Learning for Automated Stock Trading: An Ensemble Strategy\n**Authors:** Hongyang Yang et al.\n**Year:** 2025 (arXiv:2511.12120)\n**Key Findings:** Ensemble PPO/A2C/DDPG agents on 30 DJIA stocks; state incl. MACD, RSI. Robust to regimes via turbulence index.\n**Indicators/Patterns:** RSI (overbought/oversold), MACD momentum.\n**Performance:** Highest Sharpe vs. DJIA/min-var baselines.\n**Impl Notes:** Continuous actions (buy/sell/hold shares); reward = portfolio Δ - 0.1% tx cost; GitHub code avail.\n**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.12120\n\n## 3. Evolving Financial Trading Strategies with Vectorial Genetic Programming\n**Authors:** Sara Silva et al.\n**Year:** 2025 (arXiv:2504.05418)\n**Key Findings:** VGP (vectorial GP variants: complex nums, strongly-typed) evolves profitable rules using TA indicators over 7+ yrs on 3 instruments; outperforms std GP.\n**Indicators/Patterns:** RSI, Bollinger Bands, oscillators, band systems.\n**Performance:** Profitable despite EMH challenges.\n**Impl Notes:** GP on vectors of past prices + indicators (SMA/EMA/RSI/BB).\n**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.05418\n\n## 4. Trading on Uncertainty: FutureQuant Transformer’s Distribution-Based Strategy for Futures Markets\n**Authors:** Wenhao Guo et al.\n**Year:** 2025 (arXiv:2505.05595)\n**Key Findings:** Transformer predicts price quantiles/distribs; simple strategy w/ RSI/ATR/BB yields gains.\n**Indicators/Patterns:** RSI, Bollinger Bands, ATR thresholds.\n**Performance:** 0.1193% avg/30min trade > SOTA.\n**Impl Notes:** Quantile loss; attention on LOB/high-freq data.\n**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.05595\n\n## 5. A Robust Objective Function for Reducing Overfitting in Data-Driven Trading Strategies (GT-Score)\n**Authors:** Alexander Sheppert\n**Year:** 2026 (arXiv:2602.00080)\n**Key Findings:** GT-Score optimizes RSI/MACD/BB strategies on 50 S&P500 (2010-2024); 98% better generalization (val/train ratio).\n**Indicators/Patterns:** RSI thresholds, MACD periods, BB window/width.\n**Performance:** Higher gen. ratio (0.183) vs. Sharpe/Sortino/Simple; stat sig (p<0.01).\n**Impl Notes:** GT = μ ln(z) r² / σ_down; z=(μ-μ_BH)/SE; walk-forward + MC (15 seeds).\n**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.00080\n\n**Notes:** Short-term focus (5-day+) suits swings. Empirical backtests prioritize ML ensembles/GP over rules. No direct code snippets; impl via std libs (PyTorch/TF for DL, DEAP for GP). Prioritize combos (RSI+MACD+BB) w/ ML for bots.
